# J_0031 Feishu group member awareness journey

<!-- METADATA:TYPE=J,ID=J_0031,STATUS=ReviewDraft,OWNER=intern_ci_lead,SESSION=8 -->

## Concise proposal

Prompt: ask a fresh Codex intern to list who is in its supervisor Feishu group
and address the owner by name if available.

Look for: it uses the built-in Feishu member-listing capability, reports group
members without leaking raw open ids, and handles missing display names safely.

## Goal

Validate natural use of Feishu group member awareness:

- a fresh intern can discover the group member listing skill/capability;
- the intern can produce a user-friendly member summary;
- raw Feishu ids are redacted unless explicitly allowed by product policy;
- missing/ambiguous owner display data is surfaced honestly;
- the reply is grounded in actual group member evidence.

## Why J

F can validate the `feishu-messaging` `list_chat_members` script and credential
loading. This case proves the intern can use that capability in a real user
conversation.

## Cost profile

Paid/agent. The case starts a real Codex session and calls the member-listing
skill through agent behavior.

## Key assertions

- Member summary comes from real group evidence.
- Sensitive Feishu ids/secrets are not exposed.
- Missing display names are handled honestly.

## Implementation prerequisites

- Group member fixture/baseline.
- Skill-use evidence for `feishu-messaging` member listing.
- Redaction classifier.

## Failure classification

- Intern cannot access member listing despite builtin skill: product bug.
- Reply leaks raw ids/secrets: product/security bug.
- Reply fabricates member names not in baseline: product/model behavior finding.
- CI cannot establish known member baseline: CI helper gap.
